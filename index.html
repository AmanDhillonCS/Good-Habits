Good Habits

Our Vision

Good Habits is a habit-tracking application that can be used to develop good habits or get rid of bad habits.The main goal of Good Habits is to make an individual complete the tasks they have created. Each task in this context is a habit that a user wants to start or quit. The application will send out notifications at certain time intervals to keep the users on track. The app will also contain a detailed description of ongoing and past activities or habits a user has performed. To keep the peorson motivated and focused the user will receive motivational quotes at the homescreen. Moreover, it will give users a rating on how they are doing towards a habit so they get a sense of achievement and flaunt it among friends.

Intended Audience

According to Healthline, it takes a person on average about 66 days to develop a regular habit so it is made for people who have trouble developing new habits in a consistent manner, which mostly includes young adults. Furthermore, Good Habit is intended for people who lack a feeling of motivation and achievement and want something to push them towards their goals.

Postmortem

What went right in the development process?

The development process had many positive points. One of them was regular meetings and discussions, which helped in getting small tasks done faster. Regular meetings also helped in better work distribution and helped in revealing each developer's skills. Each and every group member learned something new throughout this process which will help them for their future developing endeavours.

What went wrong during the development process?

One particular big hurdle we faced was getting the database working for the persistent layer of the application. This lead to some features being push to future iterations and incomplete testing.

Another problem we faced was not being able to understand how time to allot for a particular task. This lead to some iterations with large amount of work while others had very little work involved.

What took the most time?

Database implementation took the most time. We were not able to resolve a bug related HSQLDB which lead to switching to SQLite database. At the begining of iteration 2, we didn't expect the database to take most of the iteration 2 time and decided to assign it to a single person. This decision lead to major changes such as delay in implementation of new features and minimal testing of new features. One lesson we learned from this mistake was to assign multiple people to big tasks in iteration 3 which worked well in completing tasks.

How did the project change?

In iteration 0 we initially planned the project for five people but with one person dropping we still managed to complete major functionalities of the app and no major changes were made to the app. Major features like adding/deleting a habit were still implemented. Features with low and medium priority like Dark mode and Daily task schedule were dropped for future iteraion.
